Before You Begin

End Goals vs. Means Goals

Understanding this distinction is the key to making the 4MIQ truly powerful. Your answers must come from soul — not ego.

End Goal

The Final Destination

The ultimate experiences, feelings, and states of being you desire. They answer: "What do I truly want to feel and experience in my life?" These are the things that make life worth living.

Deep love & connectionCreative flowAdventureHelping othersFinancial freedom
Means Goal

The Vehicle — Not The Destination

The methods or things you believe you need to achieve your end goals. Ask "why?" and the answer is always the real goal.

Get a promotionEarn $200k/yrGet a law degreeLose 20 lbs

Why This Matters

1

Avoid The "Stupid" Trap. Spending your life doing things you don't like simply to keep going — so you can keep doing things you don't like. End goals keep the path joyful.

2

Tap Into True Motivation. End goals connect directly to your heart. They are fueled by passion and intrinsic desire — not logic and grind.

3

Open Up To More Possibilities. One means goal closes you off. One end goal opens everything.

"By focusing on the end experiences you want to have, you create a blueprint for your soul that is authentic and deeply motivating."

Question 1 of 4

Question 01

How Do You Want To Experience Life?

Define the experiences, feelings, and states of being you want to cultivate. Think in end goals — not means.

How To Think About This

Close your eyes for a moment. Imagine your life with no financial limits, no fear, no judgment. What are you doing? How do you feel waking up each morning? What relationships surround you? Write whatever comes — feelings, places, moments, states of being. There is no wrong answer here.

Question 02

How Do You Want To Contribute?

Your life is not just about you. It is about the lives of everyone you touch. How do you want to give back?

How To Think About This

Think about the ripple effect of your life. Who do you want to have impacted? What legacy do you want to leave? Your contribution can be through your creations, your time, your service, your knowledge, your money, or your connections.

Question 3 of 4

Question 03

How Do You Want To Grow?

Now that you know your experiences and contributions — where must you grow to be able to hold them?

How To Think About This

Think beyond credentials. What does the version of you who has already lived those experiences know how to do? Who has she become? What did she learn to hold? Think about skills, wisdom, emotional capacity, and inner strength.

Question 04  ·  The Launch Pad

Who Do You Need To Be?

Inspired by Vishen Lakhiani's 3MIQ — this fourth question is Anri's addition. Character is the launch pad for everything else.

How To Think About This

Who do you need to be in order to create these dreams — to receive them, to hold them, and to expand them? Who you are being while executing a goal determines the power of its ripple effect. She knows she deserves it. She can hold it. She can multiply it. She does not self-sabotage or run away.
