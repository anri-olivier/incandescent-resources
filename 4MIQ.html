Before You Begin

End Goals vs. Means Goals

Understanding this distinction is the key to making the 4MIQ truly powerful. Your answers must come from soul — not ego.

End Goal

The Final Destination

The ultimate experiences, feelings, and states of being you desire. They answer: "What do I truly want to feel and experience in my life?" These are the things that make life worth living.

Deep love & connection Creative flow Adventure Helping others Financial freedom
Means Goal

The Vehicle — Not The Destination

The methods or things you believe you need to achieve your end goals. They are not wrong — they just aren't the truth of what you want. Ask "why?" and the answer is always the real goal.

Get a promotion Earn $200k/yr Get a law degree Lose 20 lbs

Why This Matters

1

Avoid the "Stupid" Trap. Spending your life doing things you don't like simply to go on living — so you can keep doing things you don't like. End goals keep the path joyful.

2

Tap Into True Motivation. End goals connect directly to your heart. They are fueled by passion and intrinsic desire — not logic and grind.

3

Open Up To More Possibilities. Focusing on an end goal allows the universe to present multiple pathways. One means goal closes you off. One end goal opens everything.

Question 01

How Do You Want To Experience Life?

Define the experiences, feelings, and states of being you want to cultivate. Think in end goals — not means.

How To Think About This

Close your eyes for a moment. Imagine your life with no financial limits, no fear, no judgment. What are you doing? How do you feel waking up each morning? What relationships surround you? What adventures have you lived? Write whatever comes — feelings, places, moments, states of being. There is no wrong answer here.

Question 02

How Do You Want To Contribute?

Your life is not just about you. It is about the lives of everyone you touch. How do you want to give back to the world?

How To Think About This

Your contribution can be through your creations, your time, your service, your knowledge, your money, or your connections. Think about the ripple effect of your life. Who do you want to have impacted? What legacy do you want to leave? What does the world look like because you were in it?

Question 03

How Do You Want To Grow?

Now that you know your experiences and contributions — where must you grow to be able to hold them?

How To Think About This

Think beyond credentials and titles. Think about the skills, the wisdom, the emotional capacity, and the inner strength you want to develop. What does the version of you who has already lived those experiences know how to do? Who has she become? What did she learn to hold?
